Understanding Demographic Composition of Adopters vs Non Adopters¶

Key Findings¶

  1. When we compare the mean age, Adopters have a slightly higher mean age as compared to non-adopters. This could mean that older users are likelier to become subscribers. This makes sense as older users might be employed and have the resources to be paid members
  1. In terms of Gender distribution, Male share slightly reduces in the Adopters(90% vs 95%) as compared to Non Adopters. What is interesting is the share of Females, which is almost double as compared to the Non Adopters (10% vs 5%)

Interpretation of Results¶

From the coefficients of the best model, this is the interpretation for each column

Age - For a 1% increase in age, the odds of becoming a subscriber increases by 6.4%

Male - The odds of adoption is 2.2% higher for males, when compared to females

Avg_Friend_Age - For a 1% increase in the average age of friends, casuses an increase in 2.5% of the odds to adopt

Subscriber_Friend_Cnt - For a 1% increase in Subscriber friends, a users adoption odds goes up by 9.5%

Songs Listened - For every 1% increase in song listened, the odds of adoption goes up by by 0.5%

Loved Tracks - For every 1% increase in number of posts the odds of adoption goes up by 1.6%

For the Shouts & Tenure, a 1% increase actually decreases the odds of adoption by 0.01% & 0.02% respectively.

From the above model we can conclude that the main drivers of adoption are demographics, engagement & peer network. As a priority business strategies could shift to capitalize on these behaviours.

Takeaways¶

For the free to Fee strategy, some of the key factors that affect a user is the demographic & engagement features. As a product manager, I would devise marketing strategies that target customers who are highly engaged on the platform. Metrics for engagement can be measured by Posts, Shouts etc.

Secondly, I would segment my users based on their age. Users who are older than 25, are likelier to be employed and can be ideal candidates for the Free to Fee service.

I would also redesign the experience of the app to facilitate more shares and create a social environment. The study we have done proves that having social engagement results in premium subscription.

Lastly, the T-Test we performed, shows that having subscriber friends is also a significant contributing factor. I would encourage influencer content and playlists to be shared with users to boost subscription activity.
